# Scoping Question Bank

Select questions based on the detected research type. Never ask more than 4
total -- pick the highest-signal questions for the specific topic.

## Universal Questions (Any Research Type)

1. **Intent**: What will this research inform? A decision, a presentation,
   understanding a space, preparing to build, or something else?
2. **Known context**: What do you already know or assume? Any prior work to build on?
3. **Constraints**: Hard constraints? (timeline, mandated technology, budget,
   compliance, team skills)
4. **Success criteria**: What would make this research "done"? What does a great
   output look like?

## Technical Research

5. **Evaluation criteria**: What matters most -- performance, DX, ecosystem
   maturity, cost, or something else?
6. **Scale requirements**: What scale? (users, data volume, throughput, latency)
7. **Integration context**: What existing systems must this integrate with?
8. **Build vs buy**: Open to third-party services, or must be in-house?
9. **Maturity tolerance**: Comfortable with cutting-edge, or need battle-tested?

## Product/Market Research

10. **Target user**: Who specifically? Role, context, technical sophistication?
11. **Competitive awareness**: Specific competitors to analyze?
12. **Differentiation hypothesis**: Initial theory on what makes your approach different?
13. **Market signals**: Customer requests? Gong calls, support tickets, Slack threads to mine?
14. **Business model**: How monetized or justified? Revenue target or cost savings goal?

## Domain Research

15. **Depth vs breadth**: Full domain survey or deep on specific aspects?
16. **Expertise level**: How familiar is your team? Include foundational concepts
    or focus on advanced topics?
17. **Academic vs practical**: Prioritize academic rigor or practical implementation?
18. **Time horizon**: Current state of the art, or also emerging/future directions?

## Internal Context Questions

19. **Internal sources**: Search Slack, Confluence, Jira, Gong, Snowflake?
    Specific channels, spaces, or projects to focus on?
20. **Stakeholders**: Specific teams or people whose perspective matters?
21. **Prior decisions**: Past architecture decisions or tech choices that constrain this?

## Output Preference Questions

22. **Format**: Any specific format? (comparison table, narrative brief, decision
    doc, slide-ready bullets, annotated bibliography)
23. **Audience**: Who will read this? (just you, your team, leadership, external)
24. **Depth**: Executive summary level, working document, or exhaustive reference?

## When to Skip Scoping

Skip entirely when ALL of the following are true:
- Topic is specific and well-defined
- User provided clear context about why they need the research
- Research type is obvious from the topic
- No ambiguous constraints

When skipping, briefly state your assumptions at the start so the user can
correct course early.
